About the Community AQHI Dashboard

This dashboard provides near-real-time air-quality information for the Vancouver Coastal Health region. It uses low-cost AQ sensors deployed across the Vancouver Coastal Health Authority region to provide hyperlocal data.

What is AQHI?

The AQHI is a 1-10+ scale that communicates the health risk associated with a mixture of common air pollutants. A higher value means a greater potential for adverse health effects. Learn more at Environment Canada.

How to Read the Dashboard

  • Map tab : colour-coded markers show current AQHI.
  • Detailed View : select a sensor and pollutant to see data for the past 24 hours.
  • Sensor List : table view of all active monitors.

Data Sources & Methods

Sensor data are fetched every 15 minutes from community monitors maintained by the University of British Columbia, and calibrated using algorithms developed by Dr. Naomi Zimmerman.

Limitations

Low-cost sensors can drift and may be affected by humidity, temperature, and cross-sensitivities. While calibration reduces these errors, it is important to always follow local governance, including air quality statements.
