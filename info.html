About the Community Clean Air Spaces Dashboard

This dashboard provides near-real-time air quality information for the Vancouver Coastal Health region. It uses low-cost AQ sensors deployed across the Vancouver Coastal Health Authority region to provide hyperlocal data.

What is the Air Quality Health Index (AQHI+)?

The AQHI is a scale that helps you understand how the air around you can affect your health. It is a tool meant to guide you in making choices to protect yourself, such as spending less time outside or changing your activities when air pollution levels are high. It ranges from 1 to 10+, with 1 being the best air quality. The Province of British Columbia recommends the use of the AQHI+ (AQHI-Plus). This index is very similar to the older AQHI, but is better-suited to environments that have high particulate matter, such as those affected by wildfires.

The AQHI+ is especially important for people who are more sensitive to air pollution, offering advice for times when the air poses low, moderate, high, or very high health risks.

See the Appendix for more details on how the AQHI is calculated and the AQHI scale.

The data collected by our sensors is used to calculate the outdoor AQHI+

How to Read the Dashboard

  • Map: Colour-coded markers show current AQHI. Click a marker to see details for that sensor.
  • Graphs: Select a sensor and pollutant to see data for the past 24 hours.
  • Sensor List: Table view of all active sensors showing current pollutant concentrations.

Data Sources & Methods

Sensor data is updated from community monitors maintained by the University of British Columbia, and calibrated using algorithms developed by the research team specifically for these deployments.

Limitations

Low-cost sensors can drift and may be affected by humidity, temperature, and cross-sensitivities. While calibration reduces these errors, always follow local guidance, including official air quality statements.

Appendix

How is the AQHI calculated?

In British Columbia, the AQHI is calculated in two different ways, and the higher of the two values is reported:

  1. A combined health-risk calculation using three common air pollutants:
    1. Ozone (O3) at ground level,
    2. Fine Particulate Matter (PM2.5), and
    3. Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2).
  2. Fine Particulate Matter (PM2.5) concentration alone.

What is the scale for the AQHI?

The AQHI is measured on a scale ranging from 1–10+. Index values are grouped into health-risk categories:

  • 1–3: Low health risk
  • 4–6: Moderate health risk
  • 7–9: High health risk
  • 10+: Very high health risk
