About the Community Clean Air Spaces Dashboard

This dashboard provides near-real-time air quality information for the Vancouver Coastal Health region. It uses low-cost AQ sensors deployed across the Vancouver Coastal Health Authority region to provide hyperlocal data.

What is AQHI?

The AQHI is a 1–10+ scale that communicates the health risk associated with a mixture of common air pollutants. A higher value means a greater potential for adverse health effects. Learn more at Environment Canada.

How to Read the Dashboard

  • Map: Colour-coded markers show current AQHI. Click a marker to see details for that sensor.
  • Graphs: Select a sensor and pollutant to see data for the past 24 hours.
  • Sensor List: Table view of all active sensors showing current pollutant concentrations.

Data Sources & Methods

Sensor data is updated from community monitors maintained by the University of British Columbia, and calibrated using algorithms developed by the research team specifically for these deployments.

Limitations

Low-cost sensors can drift and may be affected by humidity, temperature, and cross-sensitivities. While calibration reduces these errors, always follow local guidance, including official air quality statements.
